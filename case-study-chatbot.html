Conversational AI & chatbot design

Takke · NLP-powered dialogue flows · 2022–2025

Overview

I designed and prototyped conversational experiences for an NLP-powered chatbot used within a product platform. The goal was to create dialogue flows that felt natural, on-brand, and genuinely useful — while working within the constraints of the underlying language model.

My role

As AI Product Designer, I owned the end-to-end design of the conversational experience: from defining the interaction model and user flows, through to shaping prompts, states, fallbacks, and error handling. I worked alongside engineers to understand model behaviour and iterated based on both qualitative feedback and quantitative evaluation metrics.

Design process

  • Discovery & mapping — Mapped user intents, dialogue states, and transition logic. Identified where the chatbot added value vs. where users needed human support or alternative flows.
  • Flow design — Designed conversation flows in low and high fidelity, including branching logic, confirmation states, and graceful degradation when the model was uncertain.
  • Prompt design — Shaped system prompts and user-facing copy to align with brand voice and user expectations. Tested variations to improve clarity and relevance.
  • Prototyping & iteration — Built interactive prototypes to test flows; iterated based on internal feedback and evaluation metrics (BLEU, ROUGE) used to measure response quality.
  • Engineering collaboration — Worked closely with engineers to understand model limitations, latency, and failure modes. Designed interfaces that surfaced loading states, retry options, and fallback paths.

Key considerations

Conversational AI design sits at the intersection of UX, copy, and model behaviour. I focused on: transparency (users understanding what the system can and can't do), error recovery (clear fallbacks and retry paths), and consistency (maintaining tone and behaviour across turns). Evaluation metrics helped validate improvements, but user feedback remained the primary signal for iteration.

Outcomes

Delivered conversational flows that were adopted in production. The work established patterns for future LLM-powered features and informed how we designed for uncertainty and model constraints across the product.
